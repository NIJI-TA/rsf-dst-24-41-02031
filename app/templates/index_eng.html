    Project description

    The project is devoted to fundamental scientific research, as well as the development of new technical solutions in the field of mathematical modeling and analysis of the superposition of gravitational and magneto-electric effects in the problems of dynamics and control of the orbital and angular motion of spacecraft (SC) and SC constellations, including using methods of artificial intelligence (AI). The main object of study will be spacecraft and spacecraft constellations interacting with the geomagnetic field through Lorentz forces and moments, as well as moments due to magnetic interaction

    Project goals and objectives

    As a result of mathematical and computer modeling and subsequent analysis of the processes of the dynamics of the controlled motion of spacecraft and spacecraft constellations moving in the gravitational and magnetic fields of the Earth, a whole range of new problems will be solved, combining three urgent problems:

    Combined Motion Control

    The problem of providing effective combined (in particular, electrodynamic) methods for controlling the angular and orbital motion of a spacecraft, including spacecraft operating as part of constellations

    Artificial intelligence on board

    The problem of implementing machine learning methods and neural networks on board the spacecraft to control (including autonomous) the movement of the spacecraft under conditions of dynamic disturbances and orientation errors

    Anti-radiation protection

    The problem of providing active anti-radiation protection of the spacecraft from ionizing radiation

    Scientific novelty and relevance

    The relevance and complexity of the tasks set explain the fundamental importance and expediency of combining the efforts of specialists from different fields of knowledge and even from different countries to improve the efficiency of solving the tasks set.

    The scientific novelty of the project is determined by the fact that in the process of performing research:


    1

    New magneto-electric effects in spacecraft motion will be studied and new refined mathematical models of spacecraft motion with electrodynamic control will be developed

    2

    New approaches will be developed for the implementation of machine learning methods and the implementation of neural networks on board the spacecraft to control (including autonomous) the movement of the spacecraft and the spacecraft constellations under conditions of systematic errors and unknown limited interference caused by dynamic disturbances and orientation errors

    3

    New methods of active protection of spacecraft from ionizing radiation will be developed
